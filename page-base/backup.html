About

My research explores the intersection of humour, graphic design, and vernacular art. It is grounded in my personal collection and my design practice which focuses on everyday images and their cultural significance. I am particularly interested in understanding how humour functions in art and design and how everyday imagery contributes to the generation of meaning in visual culture. The two aspects are interrelated: humour is often embedded in daily life and vernacular design, and through visual means they can reveal insights about cultural and artistic values.
